# Yard Drive Thru - Vintage Branding Guide

## Overview
The new visual identity focuses on a **Nostalgic, Vintage Drive-In Cinema** aesthetic. It moves away from the previous "Neo-Brutalist" style to a warmer, more classic feel inspired by 1950s marquees, starry nights, and paper textures.

## Typography

### Headlines (Marquee Style)
- **Font**: `Abril Fatface` (Google Fonts) or `Rye` (Western/Vintage).
- **Usage**: Main headers (h1, h2), ticket titles.
- **Style**: Uppercase, often with text shadows to simulate depth or bulbs.

### Scripts (Brand Identity)
- **Font**: `Kaushan Script` or `Lobster` (Google Fonts).
- **Usage**: "Yard Drive Thru" logo text, special accents.

### Body & Details
- **Font**: `Merriweather` or `Libre Baskerville`.
- **Usage**: Paragraphs, dates, ticket details.
- **Style**: Classic serif for readability and vintage feel.

## Design Elements

- **Marquee Lights**: Dotted borders or circular elements mimicking light bulbs around containers.
- **Paper Texture**: Backgrounds should have a subtle grain or paper texture (CSS pattern or overlay).
- **Ticket Stubs**: "Admit One" style shapes with perforated edges (using CSS masks or border tricks).
- **Starry Night**: The hero background should be a dynamic starry sky (Three.js or CSS canvas).
- **Vintage Borders**: Double lines, ornamental corners.

## UI Components

### Buttons
- **Style**: Retro shape (slightly rounded or chamfered corners), Gold/Red background with dark text.
- **Effect**: Box-shadow to simulate physical button depth (3d press effect).

### Cards (Movies/Logistics)
- **Background**: Paper Beige or dark semi-transparent overlay.
- **Border**: Gold or Navy thin borders.
- **Shadow**: Soft, diffuse shadows (unlike the previous hard brutalist shadows).
